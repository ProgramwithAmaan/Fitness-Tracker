Winter Bulk Program

Build muscle mass and strength during the colder months with this high-volume strength training program.

Week 1-4: Foundation Phase

Day 1: Chest & Triceps
  • Bench Press 4 sets x 8-12 reps
  • Incline Dumbbell Press 3 sets x 10-15 reps
  • Cable Flyes 3 sets x 12-15 reps
  • Tricep Pushdowns 3 sets x 12-15 reps
  • Overhead Tricep Extensions 3 sets x 10-12 reps
Day 2: Back & Biceps
  • Deadlifts 4 sets x 6-8 reps
  • Pull-ups 3 sets to failure
  • Bent-over Rows 3 sets x 8-12 reps
  • Barbell Curls 3 sets x 10-12 reps
  • Hammer Curls 3 sets x 12-15 reps
Day 3: Legs & Shoulders
  • Squats 4 sets x 8-12 reps
  • Leg Press 3 sets x 10-15 reps
  • Leg Curls 3 sets x 12-15 reps
  • Shoulder Press 3 sets x 8-12 reps
  • Lateral Raises 3 sets x 12-15 reps

Nutrition Tips for Winter Bulk

  • Increase calorie intake by 300-500 calories above maintenance
  • Aim for 1.6-2.2g of protein per kg of body weight
  • Include complex carbs like sweet potatoes, oats, and brown rice
  • Don't neglect healthy fats from nuts, avocados, and olive oil
  • Stay hydrated even in colder weather

Summer Shred Program

Burn fat and define your muscles with this high-intensity circuit training program perfect for summer.

Week 1-4: Fat Burning Phase

Day 1: Full Body Circuit
  • Burpees 45 seconds work, 15 seconds rest
  • Kettlebell Swings 45 seconds work, 15 seconds rest
  • Mountain Climbers 45 seconds work, 15 seconds rest
  • Jump Squats 45 seconds work, 15 seconds rest
  • Plank 45 seconds work, 15 seconds rest
  • Repeat circuit 3-4 times
Day 2: Upper Body & Core
  • Push-ups 3 sets to failure
  • Dumbbell Rows 3 sets x 12-15 reps
  • Shoulder Taps 3 sets x 20 reps
  • Russian Twists 3 sets x 20 reps
  • Bicycle Crunches 3 sets x 30 seconds
Day 3: Cardio & Lower Body
  • HIIT Sprints 30 seconds sprint, 90 seconds walk x 8 rounds
  • Lunges 3 sets x 15 reps per leg
  • Glute Bridges 3 sets x 20 reps
  • Calf Raises 3 sets x 25 reps
  • Jump Rope 5 minutes continuous

Nutrition Tips for Summer Shred

  • Create a calorie deficit of 300-500 calories below maintenance
  • Prioritize lean protein sources like chicken, fish, and tofu
  • Increase water intake to stay hydrated in warmer weather
  • Focus on fiber-rich vegetables to stay full
  • Limit processed foods and sugars

Spring Revitalize Program

Refresh your routine with this balanced program that combines strength, flexibility, and outdoor activities.

Week 1-4: Renewal Phase

Day 1: Strength & Mobility
  • Full Body Warm-up 10 minutes dynamic stretching
  • Goblet Squats 3 sets x 12-15 reps
  • Push-ups 3 sets to failure
  • Bent-over Rows 3 sets x 12-15 reps
  • Yoga Flow 15 minutes
Day 2: Outdoor Cardio
  • Brisk Walking/Jogging 30-45 minutes outdoors
  • Park Bench Step-ups 3 sets x 15 reps per leg
  • Tricep Dips 3 sets x 12-15 reps
  • Walking Lunges 3 sets x 20 steps
Day 3: Active Recovery
  • Light Cycling 30 minutes moderate pace
  • Full Body Stretch 20 minutes focused stretching
  • Foam Rolling 10 minutes targeting tight areas
  • Deep Breathing Exercises 5 minutes mindfulness practice

Nutrition Tips for Spring Revitalize

  • Focus on fresh, seasonal produce
  • Incorporate detoxifying foods like leafy greens and citrus
  • Stay at maintenance calories or slight deficit
  • Hydrate with infused waters and herbal teas
  • Include probiotic-rich foods for gut health

Fall Endurance Program

Build stamina and prepare for indoor training with this endurance-focused program as temperatures drop.

Week 1-4: Endurance Building

Day 1: Long Distance Cardio
  • Running or Cycling 45-60 minutes at steady pace
  • Heart Rate Zone Training Maintain 65-75% of max HR
  • Cool Down Walk 10 minutes gradual slowing
  • Full Body Stretch 15 minutes post-cardio
Day 2: Strength Endurance
  • Circuit Training 3 rounds of the following:
  • Bodyweight Squats 20 reps
  • Push-ups 15 reps
  • Plank 60 seconds
  • Jumping Jacks 30 reps
  • Rest 90 seconds between rounds
Day 3: Interval Training
  • Warm-up 10 minutes light cardio
  • High-Intensity Intervals 8 rounds of:
  • Sprint/High Effort 30 seconds
  • Active Recovery 90 seconds
  • Cool Down 10 minutes light activity

Nutrition Tips for Fall Endurance

  • Increase complex carbohydrates for sustained energy
  • Include healthy fats for joint health and inflammation control
  • Stay hydrated even as temperatures cool
  • Focus on iron-rich foods for oxygen transport
  • Incorporate anti-inflammatory foods like turmeric and ginger
